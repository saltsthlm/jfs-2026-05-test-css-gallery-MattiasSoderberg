Tech District Overnight Blackout

Residents of the city’s technology hub woke up to a complete blackout early Thursday after an unexplained power surge overloaded several substations. Authorities reported that the outage began around 2:15 a.m., leaving thousands without electricity for nearly four hours. Emergency crews worked through the night to restore power while investigators examined whether a malfunction or cyber interference caused the surge. By morning, power had returned to most buildings, but officials warned that a full investigation could take days.

Coastal Tremor Swarm Detected

Geologists have reported an unusual cluster of minor tremors off the coast, prompting increased monitoring of the region’s fault lines. Over 30 small earthquakes were recorded within a 12-hour period, none strong enough to cause damage. Experts say such swarms are not uncommon but are carefully tracked to determine whether they could precede a larger event. Emergency agencies stated there is currently no cause for alarm but advised residents to review earthquake preparedness guidelines.

Highway Closed After Sinkhole

A section of the main highway was abruptly closed today after a large sinkhole opened in the middle of the roadway during rush hour. Witnesses reported seeing the pavement crack before a portion collapsed, forcing traffic to halt immediately. Transportation officials diverted vehicles and confirmed that no injuries were reported. Engineers are now assessing underground water lines and soil stability to determine what caused the collapse.
